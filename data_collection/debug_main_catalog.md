# 2025-2026 Undergraduate Catalog

Carnegie Mellon University is accredited by the Middle States Commission on Higher Education. The university is authorized to operate in several states.

[www.cmu.edu](http://www.cmu.edu/)

## Carnegie Mellon University’s Undergraduate Catalog is an annual publication that outlines the university’s academic programs and degrees, course descriptions, and relevant policies and services.

While the information presented reflects the university’s offerings as of August 2025, updates may occur throughout the academic year. For the most current program requirements, students are encouraged to consult directly with their academic department.

Questions or suggested changes to the catalog may be directed to [catalog@andrew.cmu.edu](mailto:catalog@andrew.cmu.edu). Please note: once the PDF version is finalized and archived, updates will only be made to the online version.

The next edition of the catalog will be published in August 2026. In the meantime, newly approved courses will be listed in the [Schedule of Classes](https://enr-apps.as.cmu.edu/open/SOC/SOCServlet), published each November and April.

##### Policy Statement

_Carnegie Mellon University does not discriminate in admission, employment, or administration of its programs or activities on the basis of race, color, national origin, sex, disability, age, sexual orientation, gender identity, pregnancy or related condition, family status, marital status, parental status, religion, ancestry, veteran status, or genetic information. Furthermore, Carnegie Mellon University does not discriminate and is required not to discriminate in violation of federal, state, or local laws or executive orders._

_The university’s [Discriminatory and Sexual Misconduct Policy](https://www.cmu.edu/policies/administrative-and-governance/sexual-misconduct/index.html) contains grievance procedures that provide for the prompt and equitable resolution of Complaints alleging any action which would be prohibited by this Policy._

_Inquiries concerning the application of and compliance with this statement should be directed to the Office for Institutional Equity and Title IX, Carnegie Mellon University, 5000 Forbes Avenue, Pittsburgh, PA 15213, telephone 412-268-7125._

_Obtain general information about Carnegie Mellon University by calling 412-268-2000._
